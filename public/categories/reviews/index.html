    Some notes from a concert

    January 26, 2012
    Reviews

    Took the time to go to a concert of contemporary music tonight, a rare pleasure for me these days. Psappha, at the Royal Conservatoire of Scotland, playing a concert of largely new work. Sean Friar’s Scale 9 which opened the programme was likeable and energetic, a sort of andante and allegro, or rather andante and blues, in a post- (very-post-) Gershwin vein. Nice to see an ensemble conducting themselves. I like my flavours strong and simple, and Francesca Le Lohé’s Blind Men and an Elephant was a little too detailed and finely wrought for my taste. ...
